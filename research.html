Research Summary

I am interested in developing socially assistive robots that aid the development of important social skills. This requires the ability to diagnose the current skill state and affectie states of the agents involved as well as being able to respond in an intelligent and personalized manner.

Research Projects

  • The Detection of Social Dominance in Children

    I have developed a machine learning classifier to automatically determine whether a child is 'high' or 'low' in social dominance.

    As robots become more widely used in educational contexts, they need to be able to understand group dynamics and be able to engage many children in social interactions. One important factor in group interactions is social dominance, which is expressed through both verbal and nonverbal behaviors. I explored a method for determining whether a child in a group interaction is 'high' or 'low' in social dominance based on domain-independent verbal and nonverbal behaviors. I implemented both Support Vector Machines and Logisic Regression models with 89% and 85% mean classification accuracies, showing that it is possible to successfully classify social dominance in children based on domain-independent behavioral features. This approach is novel for its application to children, the use of domain-independent features, and the success in classifying social dominance in children independent of group composition.
